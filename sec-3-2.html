Section 3.2 Intercepts, Solutions, and Factors

Activity 3.2.1. A Typical Quadratic Graph.

(a)

Verify that \(y=(2x-7)(x+1)\) is a quadratic equation. (Hint: Expand the right side.)

(b)

Graph \(y=(2x-7)(x+1)\) with technology. If you are using a graphing calculator, use the friendly window

\begin{gather*} \text{Xmin} = -9.4,~~~\text{Xmax} = 9.4\\ \text{Ymin} = -10,~~~\text{Ymax} = 10 \end{gather*}

Sketch the graph on the grid.

(c)

Use the Trace feature to locate the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph.

(d)

Solve the equation

\begin{equation*} (2x-7)(x+1)=0 \end{equation*}

(e)

Explain how your answers to parts (c) and part (d) are related.

Activity 3.2.2. X-Intercepts.

(a)

Graph the three equations below in the standard window, and sketch the graphs on the grid at right. What do you notice about the \(x\)-intercepts?

  1. \(\displaystyle y=x^2-2x+15\)

  2. \(\displaystyle y=3(x^2-2x+15)\)

  3. \(\displaystyle y=0.2(x^2-2x+15)\)

Multiplying a quadratic expression by a constant does not change the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph.

(b)

Solve by factoring \(~~20x^2-40x-700 = 0\text{.}\)

(c)

Graph \(~~y=0.1(x^2+9x-360)~~\) on your calculator, using the ZInteger setting.

(d)

Locate the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph.

(e)

Use the \(x\)-intercepts to write the quadratic expression in factored form. (Do not try to factor the expression!)

Subsection 3.2.2 Check Your Understanding

  1. Delbert says that the solutions of the equation \(4(x-2)(x+1)=0\) are \(x=4,~x=2\) and \(x=-1\text{.}\) Do you agree? Why or why not?

  2. If the perimeter of a rectangle is 56 inches, and its width is \(w\) inches, write an expression for its length.

  3. How can you use a graph to factor a quadratic expression?

  4. In Method 2 of the Quadratic Model, what did the two variables on the graph represent? What happened to the second variable as you increased the first variable?

Subsection 3.2.3 Wrap Up

In this Lesson, we worked on the following skills and goals related to quadratic models:

  • Solve quadratic equations by factoring

  • Find a quadratic equation with given solutions

  • Find the \(x\)-intercepts of a parabola

  • Solve problems involving perimeter and area or the Pythagorean theorem
