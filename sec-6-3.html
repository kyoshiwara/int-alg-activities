Section 6.3 Rational Exponents

Activity 6.3.1. Kepler's Law.

Kepler's law gives a relation between the period \(p\) of a planet's revolution and its average distance \(a\) from the sun:

\begin{equation*} p^2=Ka^3 \end{equation*}

where \(K=1.243 \times 10^{-6}\text{,}\) \(p\) is in millions of miles, and \(a\) is in years.

(a)

Solve Kepler's law for \(a\) in terms of \(p\text{.}\) Write your equation in two ways: using a fractional exponent, and using a radical.

Activity 6.3.3. Laws of Exponents.

(a)

Simplify by using the laws of exponents. Write your answers using radicals.

  1. \(\displaystyle x^{3/4}x^{5/4}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \left(x^{3/2}\right)^{5/2}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{z^{1/3}}{z^{2/3}}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{w^{1/5}w^{-3/5}}{w^{-4/5}}\)

(b)

Simplify by using the laws of exponents. Write your answers using radicals.

  1. \(\displaystyle b^{1/3}b^{2/5}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{a^{5/4}}{a^{-4/3}}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \left(-3c^{4/3}\right)^{-2}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{\left(a^{3/2}b^{-1/4}\right)^{1/3}}{a^{3/8}}\)

Subsection 6.3.1 Check Your Understanding

  1. What does the notation \(a^{0.6}\) mean?

  2. Explain how to evaluate the function \(f(x)=x^{-3/4}\) for \(x=625\text{,}\) without using a calculator.

  3. Explain why \(~x\sqrt{x} = x^{1.5}\text{.}\)

  4. Delbert says that to compute \(a^{m/n}\) you have to do the power first, and Francine says you have to do the root first. Who is correct?

Subsection 6.3.2 Wrap-Up

In this Lesson, we worked on the following skills and goals related to powers and roots:

  • Evaluate powers with rational exponents

  • Convert between radical and exponential notation

  • Graph and analyze power functions with rational exponents

  • Solve equations involving rational exponents

  • Simplify expressions using the laws of exponents
