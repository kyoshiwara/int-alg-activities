Activity 10.2.1. Weights of Animals.

(a)

The table shows the mass, or weights, of different animals. Try to plot all these values on a number line. Why is that difficult?

Mass (kg) 0.004 4 80 300 5400 70,000

(b)

Instead, take the log of each animal's weight, then plot those values:

Activity 10.2.2. Richter Scale.

On April 30, 1986, an earthquake in Mexico City measured 7.0 on the Richter scale. On September 21 a second earthquake, measuring 8.1, hit Mexico City. How many times stronger was the September quake than the one in April?

(a)

Let \(A_1\) denote the amplitude of the April quake, with \(M_1 = 7.0\text{.}\)

Let \(A_2\) denote the amplitude of the September quake, with \(M_2 = 8.1\)

Use the formula \(M = \log_{10} \left(\dfrac{A}{A_0}\right)\) to calculate \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) (in terms of \(A_0\) :

Solve for \(A_1\text{:}\) \(~~~ M_1 = \log_{10} \left(\dfrac{A_1}{A_0}\right)\)

Solve for \(A_2\text{:}\) \(~~~ M_2 = \log_{10} \left(\dfrac{A_2}{A_0}\right)\)

(b)

Finally, compute the ratio \(~\dfrac{A_2}{A_1}~\) and answer the question in the problem.
