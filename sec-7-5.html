Section 7.5 Applications of Exponential Functions

Activity 7.5.1. Doubling-Time.

(a)

Lately the inflation rate in Freedonia has been 8% annually. ("Inflation" means that prices are rising by 8% per year.) Write a formula for the price \(P\) of a $5 item as a function of time, \(t\text{.}\)

(b)

How long will it take for the price to double from $5 to $10? (Round to the nearest year.)

(c)

Use technology to graph your function in the window for \(0 \le x \le 100\text{,}\) and sketch the curve on the grid.

(d)

Use your graph to complete the table below. How long will it take the price to double from $10 to $20? From $20 to $40? (Round to the nearest year.)

\(t\) \(0\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\)
\(P\) \(5\) \(10\) \(20\) \(40\)

(e)

Add the horizontal lines \(~y=10,~y=20~\) and \(~y=40~\) to your graph. Use the intersect feature to verify that the price doubled from $5 to $10, from $10 to $20, and from $20 to $40 in equal periods of time.

(f)

Use algebra to answer the question: What is the doubling time, rounded to four decimal places?

Activity 7.5.2. Compound Interest.

If \(P\) dollars is invested at an annual interest rate \(r\) (expressed as a decimal) compounded \(n\) times yearly, the balance of the account \(A\) after \(t\) years is given by

\begin{equation*} A = P \left( 1 + \dfrac{r}{n} \right)^{nt} \end{equation*}

(a)

If you invest $1000 at 10% compounded quarterly, how long will it take the balance to double?

Activity 7.5.3. Half-Life.

The half-life of a radioactive substance is the time it takes for half of the substance to decay.

(a)

Suppose we have 200 grams of a radioactive substance whose half-life is 6 years. Complete the table and sketch a graph.

\(t\) \(N(t)\)
\(0\) \(\hphantom{0000000000}\)
\(6\) \(\hphantom{0000000000}\)
\(12\) \(\hphantom{0000000000}\)
\(18\) \(\hphantom{0000000000}\)
\(24\) \(\hphantom{0000000000}\)

(b)

Write and solve an equation to find the annual decay factor for the substance in part (a). Then write its decay law.

(c)

What is the annual percent decay rate?

Activity 7.5.4. Radium.

(a)

Radium-226 decays at a rate of 0.4% per year. Write a formula for \(Q(t)\text{,}\) the amount of radium-226 left after \(t\) years, in terms of \(Q_0\text{.}\)

(b)

Find the half-life, \(H\text{,}\) of radium-226. (Hint: You do not need to know the value of \(Q_0\text{.}\))

(c)

Use your answer to part (a) and the formula for \(Q(t)\) in terms of \(H\) to write another formula for \(Q(t)\text{.}\)

(d)

Show that your formulas in parts (a) and (c) are equivalent.

Subsection 7.5.1 Check Your Understanding

  1. Explain how to find the doubling time of an exponential function.

  2. Explain how to use the doubling time to sketch the graph of an exponential function.

    1. Write down the steps to solve the equation \(~1600 = 1000 \left( 1 +\dfrac{r}{4} \right)^{4t}~\) for \(r\) if you know the value of \(t\text{.}\)

    2. Write down the steps to solve the equation \(~1600 = 1000 \left( 1 +\dfrac{r}{4} \right)^{4t}~\) for \(t\) if you know the value of \(r\text{.}\)

  4. Delbert claims that if the half-life of an isotope is 25 years, it will all decay in 50 years. Is he correct? Why or why not?

Subsection 7.5.2 Wrap-Up

In this Lesson, we worked on the following skills and goals related to logarithms:

  • Find the doubling time or half-life of an exponential function

  • Write exponential functions using doubling time and half-life
