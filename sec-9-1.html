Section 9.1 Properties of Lines

Activity 9.1.1. Horizontal and Vertical Lines.

(a)

Graph \(3y=15\text{.}\)

Compute its slope. \(~~m=\)

Illustrate \(\Delta x\) and \(\Delta y\) on your graph.

(b)

Graph \(2x=4\text{.}\)

Compute its slope. \(~~m=\)

Illustrate \(\Delta x\) and \(\Delta y\) on your graph.

(c)

The slope of a horizontal line is .

The slope of a vertical line is .

(d)

Make a sketch of each line, then find its equation.

  1. A horizontal line through \((2,-4)\)

  2. The \(y\)-axis

  3. Parallel to the \(y\)-axis and including \((-1,-2)\)

  4. Perpendicular to \(x=3\text{,}\) intersecting it at \((3,5)\)

Activity 9.1.2. Parallel and Perpendicular Lines.

(a)

The slopes of several lines are given below. Which of the lines are parallel to the graph of \(y=2.5x-3\text{,}\) and which are perpendicular to it? Which are neither? Support each answer with a calculation.

  1. \(\displaystyle m=\dfrac{2}{5}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle m=\dfrac{25}{10}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle m=\dfrac{-8}{20}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle m=\dfrac{-45}{18}\)

  5. \(\displaystyle m=\dfrac{40}{16}\)

  6. \(\displaystyle m=25\)

  7. \(\displaystyle m=\dfrac{-1}{25}\)

  8. \(\displaystyle m=\dfrac{-5}{10}\)

(b)

In each part, determine whether the two lines are parallel, perpendicular, or neither.

  1. \(\displaystyle 2x-7y = 14;~~~7x-2y = 14\)

  2. \(\displaystyle x+y = 6;~~~ x-y = 6\)

  3. \(\displaystyle x=-3;~~~ 3y=5\)

  4. \(\displaystyle 2y=5;~~~ 5y=-2\)

Activity 9.1.3. Altitude of a Triangle.

(a)

Sketch the triangle with vertices \(A(-6,-3),~B(-6,3),~\) and \(~C(4,5)\text{.}\)

(b)

Find the slope of the side \(\overline{AC}\text{.}\)

(c)

Sketch the altitude from point \(B\) to side \(\overline{AC}\text{.}\) Find the slope of the altitude.

(d)

Find an equation for the line that includes the altitude from point \(B\) to side \(\overline{AC}\text{.}\) (Hint: You know a point \((x,y)\) that lies on the line.)

Activity 9.1.4. Finding a Parallel Line.

(a)

Write the equation \(2y-3x=5\) in slope-intercept form, and graph the equation.

(b)

What is the slope of any line that is parallel to \(2y-3x=5~\text{?}\)

(c)

On your graph for part (a), sketch by hand a line that is parallel to \(2y-3x=5~\) and passes through the point \((-3,2)\text{.}\)

(d)

Use the point-slope formula to write an equation for the line that is parallel to the graph of \(2y-3x=5~\) and passes through the point \((-3,2)\text{.}\)

Activity 9.1.5. Finding a Perpendicular Line.

(a)

Write the equation \(x-2y=5\) in slope-intercept form, and graph the equation.

(b)

What is the slope of any line that is parallel to \(x-2y=5~\text{?}\)

(c)

On your graph for part (a), sketch by hand a line that is parallel to \(x-2y=5~\) and passes through the point \((4,-3)\text{.}\)

(d)

Use the point-slope formula to write an equation for the line that is parallel to the graph of \(x-2y=5~\) and passes through the point \((4,-3)\text{.}\)

Subsection 9.1.2 Wrap-Up

In this Lesson, we worked on the following skills and goals related to equations and graphs:

  • Find equations for horizontal and vertical lines

  • Decide whether two lines are parallel, perpendicular, or neither

  • Find equations for parallel or perpendicular lines
