Section 6.5 Radical Equations

Activity 6.5.1. Solving Radical Equations.

To solve a radical equation, we:

  1. isolate the radical, then

  2. square both sides.

Be careful when squaring binomials. For example, why is the following calculation incorrect?

\begin{equation*} (x-4)^2 \rightarrow x^2 - 4^2 \rightarrow x^2-16~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~\alert{\text{(Wrong!)}} \end{equation*}

(a)

Square the binomials. Try to do the calculation mentally.

  1. \(\displaystyle (x+5)^2\)

  2. \(\displaystyle (x-6)^2\)

  3. \(\displaystyle (2t-3)^2\)

  4. \(\displaystyle (3v-4)^2\)

(b)

Follow the steps to solve the equation \(~2x-3 = \sqrt{4x-3}\text{.}\)

  1. Square both sides.

  2. Get zero on one side.

  3. Solve the quadratic equation.

  4. Check both solutions:

    \begin{gather*} x = 3:~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~\\ x = 1:~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ \end{gather*}

    You should find that one of the "solutions" checks, and the other doesn't. Why does this happen?

(c)

Use a graph to solve the equation \(~2x-3 = \sqrt{4x-3}\text{.}\)

Graph \(Y_1 = 2x-3\) and \(Y_2 = \sqrt{4x-3}\) on the grid below. How many solutions?

Graph \(Y_1 = (2x-3)^2\) and \(Y_2 = 4x-3\) on the grid below. How many solutions? What happened when we squared both sides of the equation? We should always check for extraneous solutions.

(d)

Sometimes we have to square both sides twice in order to get rid of all the radicals. Follow the steps to solve the equation \(\sqrt{y+4} = \sqrt{y+20} - 2\text{.}\)

  1. Isolate the more complicated radical.

  2. Square both sides.

  3. Isolate the term with the radical.

  4. Divide both sides by 4.

  5. Square both sides again.

Activity 6.5.3. The Gossamer Albatross.

A bicycle ergometer is used to measure the amount of power generated by a cyclist. The scatterplot at right shows how long an athlete was able to sustain various levels of power output. The curve is the graph of

\begin{equation*} y=500x^{-0.29}, \end{equation*}

which approximately models the data.

(a)

In this graph, which variable is the input and which is the output?

(b)

The athlete maintained 650 watts of power for 40 seconds. What power output does the equation predict for 40 seconds?

(c)

The athlete maintained 300 watts of power for 10 minutes. How long does the equation predict that power output can be maintained?

(d)

In 1979 a remarkable pedal-powered aircraft called the Gossamer Albatross was successfully flown across the English Channel. The flight took 3 hours. According to the equation, what level of power can be maintained for 3 hours?

(e)

The Gossamer Albatross needed 250 watts of power to keep it airborne. For how long can 250 watts be maintained, according to the given equation?
