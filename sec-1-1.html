Section 1.1 Linear Models

Subsection 1.1.1 Activity 1: Making and Reading a Graph

In 2000, the average sea level, \(L\text{,}\) was 24 millimeters above normal, and it has been rising at a rate of 3 mm per year.

Complete the table of values for the sea level \(t\) years after 2000.

\(t~\)(years) \(0\) \(5\) \(15\) \(20\)
\(L~\)(mm) \(\hphantom{00000}\) \(\hphantom{00000}\) \(\hphantom{00000}\) \(\hphantom{00000}\)

(a)

Write an equation for the sea level, \(L\text{,}\) in terms of \(t\text{,}\) the number of years since 2000.

(b)

  1. Graph the equation on the grid. Label the axes with the correct variables.

  2. To answer questions below, read the graph. Show your work on the graph:

    1. According to this model, what will the sea level be in 2020?

    2. How long will it be before the sea level is 6 centimeters above average? (How many millimeters in 1 centimeter?)

(c)

Now use algebra to find the answers for part (b) above. Compare your answers to the values you read from the graph.

(d)

Another global warming concern is rising temperature. Since 1950, the global mean temperature in degrees Celsius can be modeled by the equation

\begin{equation*} T = 13.9 + 0.13t \end{equation*}

where \(t\) is the number of years since 1950. What do the constants in this equation tell us about global temperature?

Subsection 1.1.2 Activity 2: A Decreasing Graph

Silver Lake has been polluted by industrial waste products. In 2000 the concentration of toxic chemicals in the water was 285 parts per million (ppm). Local environmental officials started a program to reduce the concentration by 15 ppm each year.

(a)

Complete the table of values showing the concentration, \(C\text{,}\) of toxic chemicals \(t\) years after 2000. Then write your answers as ordered pairs.

Year \(~~t~~\) \(C\) \((t,C)\)
2000 \(0\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\) \((0,\hphantom{00000})\)
2005 \(5\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\) \((5,\hphantom{00000})\)
2010 \(10\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\) \((10,\hphantom{00000})\)
2015 \(15\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\) \((15,\hphantom{00000})\)

(b)

  1. Label the axes, plot the ordered pairs on the grid, and connect them with a straight line.

  2. In one or two complete sentences, explain why you should stop the graph at the horizontal axis.

(c)

Interpret the point \((16,45)\) for this situation. (Use complete sentences!)

(d)

Use the graph to find those years when the concentration will be below 120 ppm but above 30 ppm. Show your work on the graph.

(e)

Write an equation for the concentration, \(C\text{,}\) of toxic chemicals \(t\) years after 2000.

(f)

Write and solve an inequality to verify your answer to part (d).

Subsection 1.1.3 Check Your Understanding

  1. In Activity 1b, you wrote an equation for sea level, \(L\text{.}\) What was the starting value, and what was the rate?

  2. In part (d) of Activity 1, did you evaluate an expression or solve an equation?

  3. In part (e) of Activity 1, you solved an equation by using the graph. Did you start by locating the given value on the horizontal axis, or on the vertical axis?

  4. In Activity 2, which variable goes on the horizontal axis?

  5. In part (e) of Activity 2, you solved an inequality. On which axis did your answers appear?

Subsection 1.1.4 Wrap-Up

In this Lesson, we worked on the following skills and goals related to linear models:

  • Analyze linear models given by a description in words, by a graph, by a table of values, or by an equation.

  • Write an equation for a linear model described in words, in the form:

    \begin{equation*} y=\text{(starting value)} + \text{(rate)} \times t \end{equation*}

  • Interpret an ordered pair of values for a linear model.

  • Make a graph from a table of values by choosing appropriate scales for the axes and plotting points.

  • Evaluate an algebraic expression.

  • Solve linear equations and inequalities.

  • Use a graph to evaluate an expression or solve an equation.
