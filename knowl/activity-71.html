Activity 5.4.6. Stopping Distance.

The faster a car moves, the more difficult it is to stop. The graph shows the distance, \(d\text{,}\) required to stop a car as a function of its velocity, \(v\text{,}\) before the brakes were applied.

(a)

Find a formula for \(d\) as a function of \(v\text{.}\) Hints:

  • How do you know from the graph that \(d=kv^2\) (instead of \(d=kv\) ?

  • Use a point on the graph to find the value of \(k\text{,}\) and then write the formula.

(b)

How fast was the car moving if it took 50 meters to stop?

(c)

What is the stopping distance for a car moving at 100 kilometers per hour?
