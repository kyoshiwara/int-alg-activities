Activity 1.5.1. Slope-Intercept Form.

In England, oven cooking temperatures are often given as Gas Marks rather than degrees Fahrenheit. The table shows the equivalent oven temperatures for various Gas Marks.

Gas Mark 3 5 7 9
Degrees 325 375 425 475

(a)

Plot the data and draw a line through the data points.

(b)

Calculate the slope of your line.

Estimate the \(y\)-intercept from the graph.

(c)

Write an equation that gives the temperature, \(T\text{,}\) in degrees Fahrenheit, in terms of the Gas Mark, \(G\text{.}\)
