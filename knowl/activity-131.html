Activity 8.5.2. Graphical Solution.

A florist finds that she will sell \(\dfrac{300}{x}\) dozen roses per week if she charges \(x\) dollars for a dozen. Her suppliers will sell her \(5x-55\) dozen roses if she sells them at \(x\) dollars per dozen.

(a)

Graph the demand function, \(~D(x) = \dfrac{300}{x}\text{,}\) and the supply function, \(~S(x) = 5x-55\text{,}\) for \(0 \le x \le 30\text{.}\)

Make a sketch of your graph, and label the axes.

At what equilibrium price, \(x\text{,}\) will the florist sell all the roses she purchases?

(b)

Write and solve an equation to verify the equilibrium price.
