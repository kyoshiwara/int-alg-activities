Activity 1.1.1. Making and Reading a Graph.

In 2000, the average sea level, \(L\text{,}\) was 24 millimeters above normal, and it has been rising at a rate of 3 mm per year.

Complete the table of values for the sea level \(t\) years after 2000.

\(t~\)(years) \(0\) \(1\) \(5\) \(15\) \(20\)
\(L~\)(mm) \(\hphantom{00000}\) \(\hphantom{00000}\) \(\hphantom{00000}\) \(\hphantom{00000}\) \(\hphantom{00000}\)

(a)

Write an equation for the sea level, \(L\text{,}\) in terms of \(t\text{,}\) the number of years since 2000.

(b)

  1. Graph the equation on the grid. Label the axes with the correct variables.

  2. To answer the questions below, read the graph. Show your work on the graph:

    Note.

    Have students draw dashed lines on the graph, as in Example 1.7.

    1. According to this model, what will the sea level be in 2020?

    2. How long will it be before the sea level is 6 centimeters above average? (How many millimeters are there in 1 centimeter?)

(c)

Now use algebra to find the answers for part (b) above. Compare your answers to the values you read from the graph.
