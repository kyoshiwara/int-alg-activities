Activity 6.2.1. Maximum Speed for Ships.

When a ship moves through the water, it creates waves that impede its own progress. Because of this resistance, there is an upper limit to the speed at which a ship can travel. This maximum speed, or hull velocity, is given in knots by

\begin{equation*} v_{max} = f(L) = 1.3 L^{1/2} \end{equation*}

where \(L\) is the length of the vessel in feet.
