Begin by trying to eradicate the erroneous version of the quadratic formula, \(x=-b \pm \dfrac{sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\text{.}\) Also point out the distinction between a quadratic equation and the quadratic formula. The treatment of \(x\)-intercepts in the reading is a nice way to illustrate the quadratic formula and lead into the discriminant. Note that we do not treat complex numbers yet, other than to acknowledge their existence. (Some students already know about complex numbers, so it is best to mention briefly, but postpone further discussion for later.)
