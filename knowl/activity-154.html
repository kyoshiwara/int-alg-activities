Activity 9.4.3. Standard Form.

(a)

Follow the steps to write the equation

\begin{equation*} 4x^2-6y^2+16x-12y-14=0 \end{equation*}

in standard form.

Collect the \(x\)-terms and \(y\)-terms together.

\begin{equation*} \fillinmath{XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX} = 14 \end{equation*}

Factor out the coefficients of \(x^2\) and \(y^2\text{.}\) Be careful: you are factoring a negative number from the \(y^2\)-terms.

\begin{equation*} \fillinmath{XXXXXXXXXXXXXXXX} = 14 \end{equation*}

Complete the square in \(x\) and in \(y\text{.}\) Be careful! Notice that you have really added \(4(4) = 16\) to the \(x\)-terms and \(-6(1) = -6\) to the \(y\)-terms on the left side of the equation.

Simplify each side.

Divide both sides by the constant, and simplify.
