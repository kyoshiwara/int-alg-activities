Task 10.2.2.a.

Let \(A_1\) denote the amplitude of the April quake, with \(M_1 = 7.0\text{.}\)

Let \(A_2\) denote the amplitude of the September quake, with \(M_2 = 8.1\)

Use the formula \(M = \log_{10} \left(\dfrac{A}{A_0}\right)\) to calculate \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) (in terms of \(A_0\) :

Solve for \(A_1\text{:}\) \(~~~ M_1 = \log_{10} \left(\dfrac{A_1}{A_0}\right)\)

Solve for \(A_2\text{:}\) \(~~~ M_2 = \log_{10} \left(\dfrac{A_2}{A_0}\right)\)
