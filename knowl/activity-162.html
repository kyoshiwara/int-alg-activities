Activity 10.1.2. Making a Choice.

Hick's Law says that the time, \(T\text{,}\) required for a person to make a choice from a list or menu of \(n\) items is proportional to \(\log_2 {n+1}\text{.}\) A classic study measured the amount of time shoppers took to choose a flavor of jam from a display of 6 flavors and from a display of 24 flavors. Hick's Law is used by restaurants and web-designers in making menus, and by survey companies in designing multiple-choice questionaires.
