Students are often confused because the symbol for logarithm, "log" looks like a word, instead of a mathematical symbol like \(\sqrt{\hphantom{0}}\text{.}\) Explain that \(\log_2{x}\) means "find an exponent on base 2 that gives \(x\text{,}\)" just as \(\sqrt{x}\) means "find a number that when squared gives \(x\text{.}\)" It may help to do several examples mentally before turning to the calculator.
