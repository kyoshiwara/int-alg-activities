In this Lesson we define powers of the form \(a^{1/n}\text{,}\) use the laws of exponents on such powers, and convert expressions from radical to exponential form and vice versa. Note that we do not yet consider powers with exponents \(m/n\text{;}\) those come in the next Lesson. We briefly discuss irrational numbers and the roots of real numbers. Finally, we consider equations and power functions that involve \(a^{1/n}\text{.}\)
