In this Lesson we use the product and quotient rules for radicals to simplify radical expressions, including rationalizing the denominator. Begin by explaining the distinction between evaluating a radical (e.g. \(\sqrt{45} \approx 6.708\)) and simplifying a radical (\(\sqrt{45} = 3\sqrt{5}\)). You might want to have students work on the Activities independently or in groups, and help individuals when they get stuck. Activity 1 is devoted to simplifying radicals, while Activity 2 considers operations on radicals.
