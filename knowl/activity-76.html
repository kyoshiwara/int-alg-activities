Activity 5.6.2. Solving Absolute Value Equations.

We'll solve the equation

\begin{equation*} \abs{x-3} = 5 \end{equation*}

in three different ways: with a graph, wth a number line, and with algebra.

(a)

Graph \(y=\abs{x-3}\) with technology, and copy the graph onto the grid below.

Sketch the horizontal line \(y=5\) on your graph.

(b)

Find the points on the graph of \(y=\abs{x-3}\) that have \(y=5\text{.}\) (How many points are there?) What are the \(x\)-coordinates of those two points? These are the solutions,

(c)

Next we'll use a number line to solve the equation. Translate the equation \(\abs{x-3} = 5\) into a sentence about distance.

(d)

Locate 3 on the number line below, and find two points at a distance of 5 units from 3.

(e)

What are the \(x\)-coordinates of those two points?

(f)

Finally, we'll use algebra to sove the equation. Recall the definition of absolute value:

\begin{align*} \abs{x} = \begin{cases} x \amp \text{if } x\ge 0\\ -x \amp \text{if } x\lt 0 \end{cases} \end{align*}

Now substitute \(x-3\) for \(x\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \abs{x-3} = \begin{cases} x-3 \amp \text{if } x-3\ge 0\\ -(x-3) \amp \text{if } x-3\lt 0 \end{cases} \end{align*}

(g)

Thus, the equation \(\abs{x-3} = 5\) has two parts:

\begin{equation*} x-3=5~~~~~~~~~~~~~ \text{and} ~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~ -(x-3)=5 \end{equation*}

Solve each equation to get two solutions.

(h)

Solve the equation \(\abs{x+6} = 2\) in three different ways:

  1. By graphing \(y=\abs{x+6}\text{.}\)

  2. By using a number line.

  3. By using algebra.
