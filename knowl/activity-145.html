Activity 9.2.6. Standard Form.

The standard form for a circle is useful because we can see the center and the radius right in the equation, so we can graph the circle.

(a)

State the center and radius of the circle \(~(x+3)^2 + (y+2)^2 = 16\text{.}\)

(b)

Graph the circle on the grid.

(c)

Expand the left side of the equation, and write it in general quadratic form:

\begin{equation*} ax^2 + bxy + cy^2 + dx + ey + f = 0 \end{equation*}

(d)

To convert an equation to standard form we complete the square. Follow the steps to convert the equation

\begin{equation*} x^2 + y^2 - 14x + 4y = -25 \end{equation*}

to standard form.

Collect the \(x\)-terms together and the \(y\)-terms together.

Complete the square for \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\)

Write each expression as a perfect square and simplify the right side.
