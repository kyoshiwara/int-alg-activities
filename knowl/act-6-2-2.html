Activity 6.2.2. Heart Rate.

An animal's heart rate is related to its size or mass, with smaller animals generally having faster heart rates. The heart rates of mammals are given approximately by the power function

\begin{equation*} H=f(m)=k m^{-1/4} \end{equation*}

where \(m\) is the animal's mass in kilograms and \(k\) is a constant.

Note.

Ask students whether they expect smaller animals to have slower or faster heart rates than larger ones, and what that means for the function \(H=f(m)\text{.}\) Then ask them how they know that \(H=k m^{-1/4}\) is a decreasing function.

(a)

A typical human male weighs about 70 kilograms and has a resting heart rate of 70 beats per minute. Find the constant of proportionality, \(k\text{,}\) and write a formula for \(H\) as a function of \(m\text{.}\)
