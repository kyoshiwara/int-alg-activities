In this Lesson, the slope-intercept formula is treated very briefly. Students should have mastered the slope-intercept form in elementary algebra, and it has already appeared in this course as the form for a linear model. We also review the coordinate formula for slope, \(m=\dfrac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}\text{,}\) and the notion of net change.
