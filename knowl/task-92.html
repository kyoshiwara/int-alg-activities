Task 2.4.3.a.

\begin{align*} \text{(1)}~~~~~~\amp 3y + z = 3\\ \text{(2)}~~~~~~\amp -2x + 3y = 7\\ \text{(3)}~~~~~~\amp 3x + 2z = -6 \end{align*}

Notice that there is no \(x\)-term in Equation (1), so it can serve as Equation (A), and Step 2 is done. What is the smart choice for Step 3?
