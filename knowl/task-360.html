Task 6.4.1.d.

To simplify the square root of an odd power of \(x\text{,}\) we factor off \(x\text{.}\) For example,

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{x^{13}} = \sqrt{x^{12} \cdot x} = \sqrt{x^{12}} \cdot \sqrt{x} = x^6 \sqrt{x} \end{equation*}

Similarly, for cube roots, we factor out a power whose exponent is a multiple of 3.

\begin{equation*} \sqrt[3]{x^8} = \sqrt[3]{x^6 \cdot x^2} = \sqrt[3]{x^6} \cdot \sqrt[3]{x^2} = x^2 \sqrt[3]{x^2} \end{equation*}

  2. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{27w^9}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{80a^5 b^4}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{72b^{16}c^{15}}\)

  5. \(\displaystyle \sqrt[3]{32 m^{11}}\)

  6. \(\displaystyle \sqrt[3]{64 w^4 z^2}\)

  7. \(\displaystyle \sqrt[3]{81 a^{12} b^8}\)

  8. \(\displaystyle \sqrt[3]{40x^{14} y^9}\)
