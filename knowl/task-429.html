Task 7.3.2.b.

From the examples, we see that a logarithm is just an exponent. In fact,

\begin{equation*} \log_b {x} = y ~~~~~~ \text{if and only if} ~~~~~~ b^y = x \end{equation*}

Convert each exponential equation to logarithmic form.

  1. \(\displaystyle 10^x = 1000\)

  2. \(\displaystyle 3^x = 243\)

  3. \(\displaystyle 8^x = 1526\)

  4. \(\displaystyle v^{5/3} = 12\)

  5. \(\displaystyle 10^{1.3t} = 3M_0\)

  6. \(\displaystyle z^{-3t} = P\)
