Activity 7.1.7. Comparing Linear and Exponential Decrease.

In 2003, the Center for Biological Diversity filed a lawsuit against the federal government for failing to protect Alaskan sea otters. The population of sea otters, which numbered between 150,000 and 300,000 before hunting began in 1741, declined from about 20,000 in 1992 to 6000 in 2000.

(a)

If the population declined linearly after 1992, what was its annual rate of decrease?

(b)

Write a formula for \(L(t)\text{,}\) the number of otters \(t\) years after 1992 under linear decline.

(c)

If the population declined exponentially after 1992, a formula for the number of otters years after 1992 is \(E(t) = 20,000 b^t\text{.}\) Use the population in 2000 to write an equation and solve for \(b\text{.}\)

(d)

What was the annual decay factor for exponential decline in the otter population? What was its annual percent decrease?

(e)

Predict the number of sea otters in 2010 under each assumption, linear or exponential decline.

(f)

Graph both functions,\(L(t)\) and \(E(t)\text{,}\) on the same grid.
