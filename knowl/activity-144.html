Activity 9.2.3. The Midpoint Formula.

(a)

Plot the points \((-5,3)\) and \((3,-9)\text{,}\) and draw the line segment joining them.

(b)

Draw horizontal and vertical line segments to make a right triangle.

(c)

Now start at \((-5,3)\) and move half the horizontal distance and half the vertical distance to \((3,-9)\text{.}\) What are the coordinates of the new point?

(d)

The new point is called the midpoint of the segment joining \((-5,3)\) and \((3,-9)\text{.}\) It is half-way between \((-5,3)\) and \((3,-9)\text{.}\)

How can we find a formula for calculating the coordinates of the midpoint? When we calculate the average of two numbers, we find a number that is half-way between them. For example, if your test scores are 70 and 90, your test average is

\begin{equation*} \text{average} = \dfrac{70+90}{2} = \dfrac{160}{2} = 80 \end{equation*}
