We recommend going over these Activities with the class, using a smart board or a projector. Almost all of the skills required will be new to most students. Even students who recently completed elementary algebra have trouble with extraction of roots: although solving \(x^2=16\) is easy for them, solving \((x+3)^2=16\) is not. In particular, watch out for the mistake of solving \(A=P(1+r)^2\) by subtracting \(P\) from both sides.
