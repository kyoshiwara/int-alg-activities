Activity 7.5.1. Doubling-Time.

(a)

Lately the inflation rate in Freedonia has been 8% annually. ("Inflation" means that prices are rising by 8% per year.) Write a formula for the price \(P\) of a $5 item as a function of time, \(t\text{.}\)

(b)

How long will it take for the price to double from $5 to $10? (Round to the nearest year.)

(c)

Use technology to graph your function in the window for \(0 \le x \le 100\text{,}\) and sketch the curve on the grid.

(d)

Use your graph to complete the table below. How long will it take the price to double from $10 to $20? From $20 to $40? (Round to the nearest year.)

\(t\) \(0\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\) \(\hphantom{0000000}\)
\(P\) \(5\) \(10\) \(20\) \(40\)

(e)

Add the horizontal lines \(~y=10,~y=20~\) and \(~y=40~\) to your graph. Use the intersect feature to verify that the price doubled from $5 to $10, from $10 to $20, and from $20 to $40 in equal periods of time.

(f)

Use algebra to answer the question: What is the doubling time, rounded to four decimal places?
